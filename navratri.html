Navratri Navratri is a Hindu festival dedicated to the worship of the goddess Durga. The word "Navratri" is derived from the Sanskrit words "nava," meaning nine, and "ratri," meaning night. The festival spans nine nights and is observed in the lunar month of Ashwin (September-October) according to the Hindu calendar. Navratri typically culminates in the festival of Dussehra or Vijayadashami.

WorshipNavratri is primarily dedicated to the goddess Durga, who is believed to symbolize the divine feminine energy (Shakti). Devotees worship different manifestations or forms of Durga during each of the nine nights.The festival involves various cultural and religious celebrations, including music, dance, and fasting. Devotees often engage in special prayers, pujas, and rituals during this period.
