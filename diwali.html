Diwali Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is a vibrant and joyous Hindu festival celebrated globally. Signifying the triumph of light over darkness and good over evil, Diwali typically spans five days of festivities. Houses are adorned with colorful decorations and earthen lamps, creating a warm and illuminated atmosphere. Families come together to exchange gifts, share traditional sweets, and partake in festive meals. Fireworks and the lighting of oil lamps, or diyas, illuminate the night sky, symbolizing the victory of light over ignorance. Diwali holds cultural, religious, and social significance, fostering a spirit of unity and spreading happiness and prosperity among communities.

Significance of DiwaliThe festival of Diwali symbolises the victory of light over darkness, knowledge over ignorance, good over evil, and right over wrong. It is an occasion of high spiritual value as it signifies the opening of our own true light which sparkles within us and sharing this glow with others as well.
